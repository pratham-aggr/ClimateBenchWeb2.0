About ClimateBench2

A new generation benchmark for advancing climate science through machine learning

Our Vision

ClimateBench2 builds upon previous climate modeling benchmarks to create a standardized framework that enables fair comparisons between different AI approaches to climate science problems.

Our benchmark includes a diverse range of climate datasets, carefully curated evaluation metrics, and standardized training and testing protocols. This allows researchers to focus on model development and innovation while ensuring results are comparable across studies.

By providing this common framework, we aim to accelerate progress in climate modeling, prediction, and understanding through machine learning and artificial intelligence.

Model Approaches

Explore various AI approaches for climate modeling tasks

Convolutional Neural Networks

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have proven effective for climate data due to their ability to capture spatial patterns. They work well with gridded data common in climate science, such as satellite imagery and climate model outputs.

Notable architectures include U-Net variants for downscaling tasks and ResNet-based models for weather prediction. These approaches excel at extracting local features and spatial hierarchies in climate data.

Transformer-Based Models

Transformer architectures have recently shown promising results in climate modeling by capturing long-range dependencies in both space and time. Their self-attention mechanisms help model global patterns across the entire climate system.

Variants such as Vision Transformers (ViT) and FourCastNet have been adapted specifically for geospatial and meteorological data, enabling better modeling of teleconnections and complex climate phenomena.

Recurrent Neural Networks

Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), especially LSTM and GRU variants, are well-suited for climate time series prediction. They can effectively model temporal dependencies in climate data across various timescales.

Hybrid approaches combining RNNs with convolutional layers have shown strong performance for tasks requiring both spatial and temporal understanding, such as seasonal forecasting and climate projection.

Physics-Informed Neural Networks

Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) incorporate physical constraints and equations directly into neural network architectures. This approach ensures that ML models respect fundamental climate physics while learning from data.

By embedding physical laws such as conservation principles and fluid dynamics equations, these models can achieve better generalization and physical consistency compared to purely data-driven approaches.
